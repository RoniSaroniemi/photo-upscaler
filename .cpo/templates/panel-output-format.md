# Panel Output Format

*Each panelist produces a perspective document following this structure.*

---

```markdown
# [Persona Name] Perspective

## Key Insight

[The single most important thing this persona sees that others might miss. This should be specific and actionable, not generic. 2-3 sentences.]

## Proposed Approach

[How this persona would solve the challenge. Can be a complete approach or emphasis on specific aspects that matter most from this lens. Be concrete — name technologies, patterns, sequences, not just principles. 1-2 paragraphs.]

## Reasoning

[Why this approach, from this persona's lens. What makes it superior to the obvious path? What evidence, analogies, or patterns support it? What would happen if we took the obvious path instead?]

## Risks I See

[What could go wrong — not just with this persona's proposal, but with any approach to this challenge. What's the team likely overlooking? Be specific about failure modes, not vague about "things could go wrong."]

## What I'd Prioritize

[If there's limited time or budget, what's the ONE thing to get right? What can be safely deferred to v2? What's the minimum viable version of the best approach?]

## Reaction Hooks

[2-3 specific, provocative points designed to stimulate disagreement and discussion in Round 2. These should be stated clearly enough that other panelists can agree or disagree with specifics, not generalities.

Example good hook: "We should skip building our own auth and use a third-party service, even though it limits customization."
Example bad hook: "We should think carefully about authentication."]
```

---

## Round 2: Cross-Commentary Format (Full Panel only)

In Round 2, each panelist receives ALL Round 1 perspectives and writes reactions:

```markdown
# [Persona Name] — Round 2 Reactions

## Strongest Agreement

[Which other panelist's point resonated most? Why? What does it unlock when combined with your perspective?]

## Strongest Disagreement

[Which point do you push back on most? Why is it wrong or incomplete from your lens? What would happen if we followed that advice?]

## New Idea Sparked

[What new approach emerged from reading others' perspectives that none of you proposed individually? The best panel outcomes are combinatorial — ideas that no single perspective would have produced.]

## Revised Position

[Has your perspective shifted after reading others? What would you change about your Round 1 proposal? Or if nothing changed, why does your original position hold despite the counterarguments?]
```
